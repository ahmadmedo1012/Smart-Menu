## 1. Toast Tab
- **URL**: https://toasttab.com
- **Screenshot**: `tmp/competitive-screens/toasttab.png`

### Notes
1. **Motion**: Minimal scroll animation. Play button on "Toast IQ" AI section opens video. Logo carousel (171K locations). Static hero with no parallax.
2. **Food imagery**: Absent — Toast is a POS/tech brand, not food-forward. Uses product screenshots of their handheld POS device. Device mockups with UI overlays.
3. **"How it works"**: Missing. Instead: "Built For Busy" headline + dropdown for business type (Bar, Food Truck, Cafe, etc.) + CTA form. No step-by-step flow.
4. **Typography**: Sans-serif (custom brand font). Heavy weight for headlines ("Built For Busy"). Hierarchical: H1 massive (bold) → H4 sub → body. Clean, corporate.
5. **Colors**: White background, red (#DD2241?) brand accent, dark text. Very corporate SaaS feel.
6. **RTL/Arabic**: No. No Arabic or RTL detected anywhere.

**Takeaway**: Toast is enterprise POS first, menu second. Their site sells reliability and scale, not visual menu experience.

## 2. Square for Restaurants
- **URL**: https://squareup.com/us/en/restaurants

### Notes
1. **Motion**: Section-based reveal. Expandable accordion (DisclosureTriangle) for features. Video play button in hero. Carousel for seller stories. Clean, professional.

5. **Colors**: White + green (#00A651 brand green). Clean backgrounds. Minimalist, lots of whitespace.
6. **RTL/Arabic**: No. Has region/language selector in footer (France, etc.) but no Arabic.

**Takeaway**: Square sells the full ecosystem (payments + POS + website). Simple, trusted, data-driven. Menu is a feature, not the hero.

## 6. Awwwards — Restaurant Category
- **URL**: https://www.awwwards.com/websites/restaurant/
- **Screenshot**: `tmp/competitive-screens/awwwards-restaurant.png`

### Additional detail screenshots:
- `tmp/competitive-screens/ballena-fine-dining.png` — Ballena Fine Dining (Awwwards nominee)

### Notes
1. **Design trends observed**: Full-bleed hero imagery is standard. Sites use custom serif/sans-serif pairings for "fine dining" feel. Scroll-triggered animations (reveal, fade, slide) are baseline. Dark mode is popular (Ballena, Crav Burgers use dark backgrounds).
2. **Food imagery**: Hero-dominant, full-bleed, high-quality. Often single dish hero replaces multi-photo carousels. Composition: dish as hero → text overlay → CTA below fold.
3. **Typography trends**: Editorial typography. Ballena uses elegant serif/display font for hero, sans-serif for body. Large font sizes (60-80px headlines). Lots of letter-spacing for luxury feel.
4. **Ballena Fine Dining specifically**: Dark background, serif headline, minimalist layout. Small logo top-left. Reservation/book table CTA. No price display on hero. Atmosphere > features.
5. **Common pattern**: "About → Menu → Reservations → Contact" — linear narrative for restaurant sites. Awards/badges in footer. Subtle micro-interactions on hover (link underlines, image scale).

**Takeaway**: Awwwards restaurant sites prioritize visual storytelling over utility. Inspiration for Smart Menu's visual identity but not for product page UX.

## 7. Dribbble — Digital Menu Restaurant Search
- **URL**: https://dribbble.com/search/digital-menu-restaurant
- **Screenshot**: `tmp/competitive-screens/dribbble-digital-menu.png`

### Notes
1. **UI patterns**: Mobile-first menu UIs dominate. Card-based item layouts with photo thumbnail left, name/description middle, price right. Dark mode menu designs are prominent (black background, warm food colors pop).
2. **QR code integration**: Many concepts show QR codes as design elements (scannable decoration). Placed on table tents, receipt slips, and splash screens.
3. **Color trends**: Dark backgrounds (#1a1a2e, #16213e) with warm food accents (amber, tomato red, olive green). Light mode uses clean whites with warm wood tones.
4. **Iconography**: Minimal line icons for categories (burger, pizza, drink). Emoji rarely used — custom icons preferred. Category filter as horizontal scroll.
5. **Interaction patterns**: Add-to-cart as floating button. Item detail as bottom sheet. Swipeable categories. Quantity selector as + / - circle buttons.

**Takeaway**: Dribbble concepts validate dark mode for menu apps. Card-based layouts with photo + name + price is the dominant pattern for digital menu UI.

## Cross-Cutting Analysis

| **Arabic/RTL** | No | No | No | No | No |
| **AI messaging** | Toast IQ | Mentioned | Core promise | Absent | Core promise |

## Key Findings for Smart Menu

1. **RTL/Arabic is a blue ocean**: None of the 5 direct competitors support Arabic or RTL. Smart Menu's Arabic-first positioning is a massive differentiator in the MENA market.

2. **"How it works" best pattern**: Popmenu's 4-step carousel is the clearest. Smart Menu already has "3 خطوات فقط" (3 steps) — this format works well for Arabic audiences.

3. **Social proof gap**: Competitors lead with dollar figures ($192K, +$4.5M, etc.). Smart Menu shows "500+ مطعم مسجل" and "98% رضا" — these are good but could be more specific/impactful.

4. **AI messaging is table stakes**: Popmenu and Owner both lead with "AI platform." Smart Menu may benefit from emphasizing AI-powered features (suggestions, analytics, etc.) in its messaging.

5. **Design opportunity**: Menu Tiger proves the QR menu market exists but has low design quality. Smart Menu can differentiate by offering premium visual quality + Arabic-first experience at a competitive price.

6. **Dark mode**: Dribbble data strongly supports dark mode for menu apps. Smart Menu already has it (الوضع الليلي toggle). This should remain a flagship visual feature.

7. **Food photography**: None of the SAAS competitors use food photography effectively as a design element. Awwwards restaurant sites do. Smart Menu's landing page could benefit from aspirational food imagery (high-quality dish photos) instead of purely functional screenshots.
